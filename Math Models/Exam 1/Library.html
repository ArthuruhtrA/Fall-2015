SWEN-220 Alloy Exam #1

Library Model

Instructions

Your instructor will tell you how to download the files needed for this assignment / activity / exam.

You are to model a simple library using the predefined signatures and relations in file Library.als; a theme that makes visualization a bit easier is in Library.thm.

The signatures, relations are documented, as are the facts you are to complete. If you have any questions about interpreting what is required, ask your instructor.

You are NOT to:

  • Add or modify any signatures or relations (fields),
  • Add any of your own facts. (You may optionally use predicates and/or assertions in developing your model, but remove them  before submitting your final solution.)

Brief Model Description

The signatures in the model are:

  • Book - a book kept in the library.
  • Patron - a library patron who can have books on loan while indicating others he or she wants.
  • The one and only Library, which tracks books on the shelves of the library itself, and possibly some books on reserve.

The facts are designed to ensure a consistent state, which means (among other things):

  • Books on reserve are always on the shelves (reserved books cannot be loaned).
  • All books are either on the shelves or on loan to a patron, and no book can be both shelved and on loan.
  • No two patrons can have the same book loaned to them.
  • If a patron wants a book, it must be on loan (otherwise it could be loaned to the patron that wants it).
  • No patron both have and want the same book(s).

  • Make sure that your model is free of syntax errors. Comment out those facts generating errors so that valid ones can execute.
